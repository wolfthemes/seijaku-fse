# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Freelance developers and designers buying premium WordPress themes for client projects or personal work; small agencies evaluating the catalog for reliability, breadth, and support. Split roughly equal. Context: they arrive with intent — they know what a theme marketplace is, they're comparing quality at a glance, and they're making a small but real purchasing decision. The design must convert trust before the copy does.

## Product Purpose

wolfthemes.com is the flagship storefront for WolfThemes — a Power Elite ThemeForest author with 14 years of work. It replaces the current Elementor-based site with a bespoke FSE child theme that matches the quality of the products being sold. Success = a visitor who looks at the homepage and immediately understands they're dealing with a craftsperson, not a template shop. The brushstroke logo is the primary brand texture and the north star for every design decision: if a choice doesn't coexist with it, that choice loses.

## Brand Personality

Editorial · Precise · Artisan. The site is the portfolio and the pitch simultaneously. Its warmth comes from craft — the brushstroke logo, the gold accent placed once per surface — not from decoration or copy that tries to charm. Tone is direct and unhyped. Mature voice. No enthusiasm punctuation. One or two handcrafted moments per surface; never stacked.

## Anti-references

- ThemeForest listing pages: cluttered feature grids, badge overload, orange CTAs
- Generic SaaS landing pages: hero-metric templates, big-number stats, identical card grids
- Corporate agency sites: stock photography, fake testimonials, padded copy
- Cold/purely minimal SaaS: stark black-on-white with zero warmth, no humanity
- Decorative / over-designed: gradients everywhere, glassmorphism, excessive animation
- Warm-neutral beige/cream AI default: the whole sand/paper/parchment band reads as generated

## Design Principles

1. **The brushstroke is the brand.** Every design choice must coexist with the brushstroke wordmark. If an element competes with it or makes it feel out of place, remove the element — not the logo.
2. **Space earns trust.** Negative space is not emptiness — it signals that nothing superfluous survived the edit. Use it deliberately.
3. **Typography carries the voice.** Type choices and typesetting are the brand voice made visible. The scale is deliberate; don't deviate from it.
4. **Gold once.** The accent is used at most once per section. Its rarity is the point. The moment gold appears twice on the same surface, it stops being an accent.
5. **Show, don't explain.** The themes grid IS the proof. Prioritize the work; minimize copy that describes how good the work is.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text contrast ≥4.5:1, large text ≥3:1. Full keyboard navigation. Meaningful focus indicators. `prefers-reduced-motion` respected on all animations — crossfade or instant transition as fallback. No color-only information signaling.
